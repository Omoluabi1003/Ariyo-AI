Àríyò AI Privacy & Data Use

Clear notes on how the studio handles audio streams, chat prompts, and visit analytics.

What we collect

  • Visit analytics: when you open Àríyò AI we create a random ID in your browser’s localStorage so we can count unique visitors. We send anonymous visit totals to CountAPI and, only for new visitors, a short notification via FormSubmit. No names, emails, or IP addresses are stored by us.
  • Chat prompts: Àríyò AI chat and Sabi Bible run inside Zapier-hosted bots. Anything you type in those panels is processed and stored by Zapier according to their privacy policy. We do not keep a copy of your conversations.
  • Embedded media: Radio stations, Omoluabi-managed audio files, and YouTube videos stream directly from their respective hosts. Those providers may log standard metadata (IP address, device type, play counts) when media loads.

How we use the information

  • Monitor overall traffic and performance so we can keep Àríyò AI fast and stable.
  • Spot when new listeners discover the experience and celebrate the milestone.
  • Improve prompt suggestions and cultural coverage by reviewing anonymous usage trends.

Your choices

  • Clear the localStorage entry named ariyoUserId (or use your browser’s “clear site data” option) to reset your anonymous visitor ID.
  • Mute or stop the welcome audio from the speaker button; we only preload a track after you interact.
  • Avoid or close the chat/Bible panels if you do not want Zapier to process your prompts.

Third-party services

  • Zapier for chatbot hosting.
  • CountAPI for aggregate visit counters.
  • FormSubmit to notify the creator about brand-new visitors.
  • YouTube, Omoluabi-managed audio libraries, and curated radio streams for media playback.

Each service has its own privacy commitments. Review their policies if you want the full details.
